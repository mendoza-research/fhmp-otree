  • There are two types of assets available: high quality assets and low quality assets. The high quality asset is worth 300 points to the buyer, and the low quality asset is worth 100 points.

The following will happen each period.

  • First, each seller receives a probability between 0% and 100%. This probability represents the likelihood that that seller’s asset is a high quality asset. For example, if seller A receives 64% probability, then there is 64% chance the asset will be a high quality asset equal to 300 points, and a 36% chance the asset will be a low quality asset equal to 100 points.
  • One seller’s probability does not impact the probability that the other sellers’ will receive. For example, seller A could have a probability of 96%, seller B could have a probability of 87%, and seller C could have a probability of 87%.
  • Each seller must decide how to disclose to the buyers the probability that the seller received. Each seller has two options, to disclose low detail information or high detail information:
    • Low Detail: Seller says whether the probability is either between 0%-49% or between 50%-100%. This option is no cost to the seller.
    • High Detail: Seller says whether the probability is between 0%-24%, between 25%-49%, between 50%-74%, or between 75%-100%. This option costs the seller 30 points.
  • After each seller chooses high or low detail, the seller then chooses which range to disclose to the buyer.
  • The buyers place one private bid on each seller’s asset.
  • After bids are placed, the computer will select the asset type based on the underlying probability known to the seller.
  • Each seller’s asset type is revealed to all participants, and payouts are determined.
  • This process will repeat for 20 periods.
